A key element of graduate schemes is to develop the student, and give them real-life experience of working in an organisation. Being a student can be tough, with many needing to work part-time to fund their studies or to have a social life. Not only can this eat into the time required to study, but it may be irrelevant to their course and career aspirations. There are some amazing young students out there, with some fantastic ideas, skills, that could be enhanced and developed throughout their studies, given the right platform, rather than distracted by part-time jobs.

Sites like Kaggle give experts the opportunity to analyse data sets, apply analytical expertise and skills such as machine learning in competition, however companies around the world have a number of projects, both big and small, that could benefit from student research, learning, and fresh knowledge of some exciting new skills and knowledge. So, why not give students the opportunity to work on your projects part time. Why not replace part-time jobs that take them away from their studies, and replace it with part time jobs that enhance and continue to develop them for the future.

The aim of the site is to promote students, and give them a platform to develop and share new ideas with students alike, like us, and use it to give them an opportunity to contribute to a business they can feel part of. We also aim to give them opportunities to work in different companies, in support of their degree and development, rather than having the sistractions of non-related part time jobs, which is why we want to keep developing them and giving them the opportunities that will allow them to flourish. It enables them to put forward new ideas, learn new things from the working world as they go through their university career, that can be reflected back into their studies, and does not distract them from their path and course, in achieving a bright and prosperous career.

The site also provides a platform, whereby employers can upload projects that require fresh innovation and thinking (or just need some enthusiastic support), but also can engage and encourage students throughout their studies, to when they finally come to pick an employer. This process promotes the employer, as well as sets up the student with some valuable experience, as well as develops professional relationships, that will ease the transition from study to career.
